# Wellbeing Resources

## Counseling and Mental Health
**Counseling Center** — Stevens Hall 220 — open M–F 9am–5pm.
- 6 free individual sessions per academic year, no insurance needed.
- Same-day urgent slots reserved 2pm–4pm M–F. Walk in or call ahead.
- Group therapy and workshops listed on the portal.

**After-hours crisis support:** Campus Care Line **1-800-555-CARE**
(24/7, free, confidential). Connects to a licensed clinician.

**Emergency (active risk of harm):** call **911** or campus safety
**1-800-555-SAFE**. The Counseling Center cannot triage immediate
emergencies — escalate.

## Accessibility Services
**Office of Disability Services** — Robertson Hall 110 — schedule via
the portal. Provides accommodations (extended testing time,
note-takers, assistive technology), and arranges housing
accommodations when applicable. Documentation requirements outlined
on the office website.

## Health Services
**Student Health Center** — Wellness Building — primary care,
vaccinations, women's health, sports medicine. Insurance not required
for routine visits; some procedures billed to insurance or
out-of-pocket.

## Food Insecurity
**Campus Food Pantry** — Student Union basement — open M, W, F
11am–3pm. No questions asked, no proof of need required. Bag limit
per visit.

## Title IX
Reports of sexual misconduct, gender-based discrimination, or related
concerns can be made confidentially to the Title IX Office in
Administration Hall 305 or via the anonymous online reporting form.
Counseling Center staff are confidential resources; faculty and most
staff are mandatory reporters.

## Don't promise availability you can't verify
When a student asks about a specific service slot or wait time,
recommend they contact the office directly via the listed line or
visit during posted hours — the bot does not have live schedule data
for these services.
