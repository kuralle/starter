# Academic Policies

## Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)
Students must maintain a cumulative GPA of at least 2.0 to be in good
academic standing. A GPA between 1.5 and 1.99 puts a student on
academic probation. A GPA below 1.5 may result in academic dismissal.
Students on probation must meet with their advisor before registering
for the next term.

## Add/Drop Window
- **Add deadline:** end of week 2 of the term.
- **Drop without W:** end of week 4 of the term. Refund schedule applies.
- **Drop with W (no refund):** weeks 5 through 10.
- **Withdraw with WF (no refund, grade impact):** after week 10. Requires
  Dean's approval.

## Withdrawal from a Term
A full-term withdrawal must be filed with the Registrar before the end
of week 10. Withdrawals after week 10 are recorded as WF for each
enrolled course unless documented hardship is approved.

## Leave of Absence
Students may request a Leave of Absence (LOA) for one or two terms.
LOA requests require the academic advisor's sign-off and, for students
receiving federal aid, a meeting with the Financial Aid office to
discuss aid-disbursement implications.

## Course Repeat Policy
A course may be repeated for grade improvement. Only the higher grade
counts toward the GPA, but both attempts appear on the transcript.
Courses may be repeated at most twice without Dean approval.

## Grade Appeals
Grade disputes must be filed with the instructor first, then escalated
to the Department Chair within 30 days of grade posting.
