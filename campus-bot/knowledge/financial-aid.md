# Financial Aid

## Aid Package Components
Aid packages may include any combination of:
- **Federal grants** (Pell, SEOG) — do not need to be repaid.
- **Institutional scholarships** — typically renew annually subject to
  GPA minimums.
- **Federal direct loans** (subsidized + unsubsidized) — must be repaid
  after graduation or dropping below half-time.
- **Federal work-study** — up to 15 hours/week of on-campus work, hourly
  wage applied against the term balance.

## Disbursement Schedule
Aid disburses to the student account 10 days before the start of each
term, then again at the midpoint. Any aid in excess of tuition and
fees is refunded to the student within 14 days of each disbursement.

## Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP) for Aid
Federal aid recipients must maintain:
- Cumulative GPA of at least 2.0
- At least 67% completion rate of attempted credits
- Not exceed 150% of the published program length

Failing SAP results in aid suspension. Students may appeal once per
academic year with documented mitigating circumstances.

## Payment Plans
Students who cannot pay the term balance in full may enroll in the
4-month Payment Plan. A $50 enrollment fee applies. Plans are
managed through the Student Accounts portal.

## Late Payments
Balances unpaid by the end of week 2 incur a 1.5% monthly late fee
and a registration hold on future terms until the balance is brought
current.

## Outside Scholarships
Students must report outside scholarships ≥ $500 to Financial Aid
within 30 days of award notification. Outside awards may reduce
need-based components of the package, but never institutional merit
scholarships.
